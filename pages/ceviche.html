Ceviche peruano

Description

Ceviche is a Peruvian dish typically made from fresh raw fish cured in citrus juices, most commonly lime or lemon. It is also spiced with aj√≠, chili peppers or other seasonings; julienned red onions, salt and cilantro are also added.

Because the dish is eaten raw and cooked without heat, it must be prepared fresh and consumed immediately to minimize the risk of food poisoning. Ceviche is often eaten as an appetizer; if eaten as a main dish, it is usually accompanied by side dishes that complement its flavors, such as sweet potato, lettuce, maize, avocado or cooking banana.

Ingredients

  • 2 sweet potatoes
  • 1 red onion, cut into strips
  • 1 cup fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 stalk celery, sliced
  • 1/4 cup cilantro leaves
  • 1 pinch ground cumin
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 habanero pepper, seeded and minced
  • 1 lb tilapia, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1 lb shrimp - peeled and deveined
  • 1 lettuce, separated into leaves
  • Salt and ground pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Set up vegetables
    1. Place the sweet potatoes in a saucepan and cover with water.
    2. Simmer until easily pierced with a fork; then drain and set aside to cool.
    3. Place the sliced onion in a bowl of warm water.
    4. Let stand 10 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare dressing
    1. In a blender, puree the lime juice, celery, cilantro, and cumin until smooth.
    2. Pour into a large glass bowl, and stir in the garlic and habanero pepper.
    3. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Marinate seafood
    1. Stir into the previous mixture the diced tilapia and shrimp.
    2. Set aside to marinate for 20 minutes tops, stirring occasionally.
    3. The seafood is done once it turns firm and opaque.
  4. Serve fresh
    1. Stir the onions into the fish mixture.
    2. Line serving bowls with lettuce leaves.
    3. Spoon the ceviche with its juice into the bowls and garnish with slices of peeled sweet potatoes.
