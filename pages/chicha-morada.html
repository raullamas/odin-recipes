Chicha morada

Description

Chicha morada is a beverage originated in the Andean regions of Peru. The drink is made of culli or ckolli - a Peruvian variety of purple corn (locally known as maíz morado) which is abundantly harvested along the Andes.

Consumption of this exotic refreshment dates to pre-Columbian times, prior to the establishment of the Inka Empire. The recipe in its current form can be traced back to the nineteenth century - to the writings of Juan de Arona, Carlos Prince and Camille Pradier-Fodéré.

Ingredients

  • 1 gal water
  • 15 oz dried purple corn (maíz morado)
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 tbsp whole cloves
  • 5 large lemons, juiced
  • 1 1/2 cups brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup fresh pineapple, chopped
  • 1/2 apple, chopped

Steps

  1. Go purple
    1. Place the corn, cinnamon sticks, and cloves in a large pot.
    2. Fill with water and bring the mixture to a boil.
    3. Reduce heat to medium-low; simmer for 40 minutes.
    4. Remove from heat and strain through a mesh strainer to remove spices and impurities.
  2. Serve cool
    1. Stir the lemon juice and sugar into the mixture until sugar has dissolved.
    2. Refrigerate until cold.
    3. Stir in the fruit.
    4. Serve over ice.
