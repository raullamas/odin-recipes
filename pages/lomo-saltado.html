Lomo saltado

Description

Lomo saltado is a popular, traditional Peruvian dish - a stir fry that typically combines marinated strips of sirloin (or other beef steak) with onions, tomatoes, french fries, and other ingredients. It is typically served with rice.

In his 2013 article Peru's Lomo Saltado on BBC TV…., British-Peruvian chef Martin Morales called lomo saltado one of Peru's most loved dishes and that this juicy mixture of beef, onions, tomatoes, ají amarillo paste and soy sauce sautéed in a large pan (or wok) is one of the many contributions Chinese immigration brought to Peru.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz frozen french fries
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 lb beef tri tip, sliced 1/4 inch thick
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 3 tomatoes, peeled, seeded, and sliced
  • 1 yellow chili pepper (ají amarillo)
  • 1/4 cup distilled white vinegar
  • 1 dash of soy sauce to taste
  • 2 tbsp chopped parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Get the fries
    1. Preheat the oven to 425 ℉ (220 ℃).
    2. Arrange frozen french fries in a single layer on an ungreased cookie sheet.
    3. Bake in the preheated oven until light golden, 22 to 24 minutes.
  2. Cook your meat
    1. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat.
    2. Season sliced beef with salt and pepper.
    3. Cook and stir beef in hot oil until just cooked and the juices release.
    4. Use a slotted spoon to transfer beef to a plate.
  3. Keep cooking
    1. Cook and stir onions in beef drippings in the pan until translucent, 3 to 4 minutes.
    2. Stir in tomato and ají amarillo; cook until tomato softens.
    3. Pour in vinegar and soy sauce.
    4. Add fries and beef to the pan. Cover and cook until beef is done - about 3 minutes.
  4. Serve juicy
    1. Season with salt and pepper.
    2. Sprinkle with chopped parsley to serve.
